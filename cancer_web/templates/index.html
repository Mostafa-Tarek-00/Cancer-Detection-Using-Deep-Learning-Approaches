Our Classes

Brain Tumor

Brain tumors cause symptoms like headaches, seizures, weakness. Diagnosis: imaging, biopsy. Treatments: surgery, radiation, chemotherapy. Multidisciplinary care essential for comprehensive treatment.

Melanocytic Nevi (nv)

Melanocytic nevi, commonly known as moles, are benign growths that develop from melanocytes, the pigment-producing cells in the skin.

Melanoma (mel)

Melanoma is the most serious type of skin cancer, developing from melanocytes. It can spread rapidly to other parts of the body if not treated early.

Benign Keratosis-like Lesions (bkl)

Benign keratosis-like lesions are non-cancerous growths that resemble actinic keratoses but are typically harmless.

Basal Cell Carcinoma (bcc)

Basal cell carcinoma is a common type of skin cancer that develops from the basal cells in the outer layer of the skin. It is usually slow-growing and rarely spreads to other parts of the body.

Actinic Keratoses and Intraepithelial Carcinoma (akiec)

Actinic keratoses are precancerous growths that develop on sun-exposed areas of the skin. Intraepithelial carcinoma refers to cancerous growth confined within the top layer of the skin.

Vascular Lesions (vasc)

Vascular lesions include various types of abnormalities affecting blood vessels in the skin, such as hemangiomas and vascular malformations.

Dermatofibroma (df)

Dermatofibroma is a benign skin tumor that typically presents as a small, firm bump. It is composed of fibrous tissue and can vary in color.
